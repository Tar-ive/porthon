# V3 Lead Procurement Strategy

## Strategic posture

Default strategy for Theo-like freelancers:
- Bias toward `Referral + Inbound` lanes.
- Constrain outbound intensity unless trust and follow-up discipline are healthy.
- Prioritize close-ready leads over net-new volume when cash stability is stressed.

## Lane model

- Referral lane: highest trust, best conversion leverage.
- Inbound lane: strong intent, medium effort.
- Previous-client lane: high reactivation potential, low acquisition cost.
- Outbound lane: selectively used, higher trust risk and cost.

## Procurement loops

1. Capture loop
- Every inquiry enters Notion as a lead row immediately.
- Figma comments can be promoted to leads with deterministic linking.

2. Nurture loop
- Daily queue from overdue follow-ups and high-confidence opportunities.
- Every touch sets explicit next action and next follow-up date.

3. Close loop
- Proposal-stage leads are handled with tighter SLAs and explicit owner pod transitions.

4. Recovery loop
- Weekly stale-lead sweep: re-engage or close-lost.

## Quality guardrails

- No spam bursts.
- No irreversible customer-facing sends without approval.
- Keep communication concise, specific, and context-aware.
